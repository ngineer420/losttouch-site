About losttou.ch

A small site about the people and memories I mean to hold onto, and don't always.

I started losttou.ch from a plain observation: almost everyone has a short mental list of people they've lost touch with who they'd genuinely like to hear from again. A college roommate. A cousin who moved away. A best friend from a job you left five years ago. Nobody fell out — life just got quieter between you.

Most of the internet's advice on relationships assumes the relationship is already active. There's an enormous amount written about deepening a marriage, resolving a fight, or setting a boundary with a coworker. There's much less written about the specific, slightly awkward act of reaching back across a gap of silence — what to say, whether it's weird, how to do it without sounding like you want something.

That's the gap I built this site to fill. I write every article to be read once, in one sitting, and acted on the same day. Not a 20-part course. Not a newsletter you'll forget to unsubscribe from. Just a straightforward guide, a next step, and (where it's genuinely useful) a link to a product or service that can help.

What you'll find here

  • Practical guides on reconnecting with old friends and family
  • Ideas for digging up and organizing your digital past — old photos, emails, and social media archives
  • Physical memory-keeping: keepsake boxes, letter-writing, and gift ideas
  • Honest reviews of memory-keeping and journaling tools

How the site is run

I run losttou.ch by myself. It's hosted as a plain static site with no ad networks, no invasive analytics, and no account system — just articles. Some pages contain affiliate links to products mentioned in the text (see my Affiliate Disclosure), which is how I plan to cover the site's very small hosting costs and, eventually, make it worth the time to keep writing. Affiliate links never change what I recommend — if a product is mentioned, it's because it fits the guide, not because it pays the most.
